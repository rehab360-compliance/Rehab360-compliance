## 1. Purpose

This Assessor's Guide provides the registered assessor with a structured framework for planning, conducting, and recording assessments for the **`QUALIFICATION_TITLE`** (`QUALIFICATION_ID`) at **NQF Level `[4 / 5]`**.

It ensures that all assessments are:
- Valid — assessing what they are intended to assess.
- Reliable — producing consistent results across assessors and occasions.
- Fair — free from bias and accessible to all learners.
- Sufficient — covering the full scope of required outcomes and unit standards.
- Authentic — confirming that the evidence is the learner's own work.
- Current — reflecting the learner's present competence.

## 6. Instructions to the Assessor

1. Read this guide thoroughly before the assessment day.
2. Ensure you hold a current SAQA-registered assessor qualification or are supervised by a qualified assessor.
3. Do not assess learners with whom you have a conflict of interest; report any conflict to the Assessment Coordinator before proceeding.
4. Administer each instrument in the order specified in Section 8.
5. Record all observations and evidence contemporaneously (at the time of observation).
6. Apply the marking guide in Section 10 and the decision rules in Section 12 consistently.
7. Do not share model answers or rubric criteria with learners before the assessment.
8. Retain all completed assessment records and evidence in the learner portfolio and submit to the Assessment Coordinator within **`SUBMISSION_TIMEFRAME`** of completing the assessment.

## 8. Assessment Steps

### Step 1 — Learner Briefing
- Welcome the learner and confirm their identity.
- Explain the purpose, structure, and timing of the assessment.
- Confirm the learner understands the competency/not yet competency decision.
- Obtain signed consent/briefing acknowledgement.

### Step 2 — Knowledge Assessment
- Administer the knowledge assessment instrument.
- Allow `< TIME >` for completion.
- Collect and secure the learner's responses immediately after completion.

### Step 3 — Practical Assessment
- Set up the practical task as specified in the instrument.
- Brief the learner on the task requirements without revealing assessment criteria.
- Observe the learner and complete the observation checklist (Appendix `APPENDIX_REF`).
- Do not interrupt or assist the learner during the task unless safety is at risk.

### Step 4 — Workplace / Portfolio Review
- Review the learner's portfolio against the evidence requirements in Section 9.
- Verify authenticity: cross-reference with supervisor declarations, dates, and contextual indicators.
- Record which evidence items satisfy which unit standard outcomes.

### Step 5 — Scoring and Decision
- Complete the scoring tables in Section 10.
- Apply the decision rules in Section 12.
- Record the overall decision: **Competent (C)** or **Not Yet Competent (NYC)**.

### Step 6 — Learner Feedback
- Provide the learner with written feedback using the Feedback Form in Section 13.
- Explain the decision and, if NYC, the specific gaps and re-assessment pathway.

## 12. Decision Rules

| Decision | Criteria |
|---------|---------|
| **Competent (C)** | The learner has met the minimum pass mark for the knowledge assessment **and** demonstrated all practical criteria at the required level **and** submitted portfolio evidence that meets validity, authenticity, currency, and sufficiency requirements for all unit standards. |
| **Not Yet Competent (NYC)** | The learner has failed to meet one or more of the criteria listed under "Competent". The assessor must clearly record which criteria were not met and provide specific developmental feedback. |

> **Re-assessment:** A learner who receives an NYC decision is entitled to one (1) re-assessment opportunity within `< RE-ASSESSMENT TIMEFRAME >`. The assessor must specify which instruments and criteria require re-assessment; the learner does not need to repeat instruments already passed.

## 15. Appeals Process

Learners who wish to appeal an assessment decision must:

1. Submit a written appeal to the **`APPEALS_CONTACT_ROLE`** within **`APPEALS_TIMEFRAME`** of receiving the assessment outcome.
2. Clearly state the grounds for the appeal (procedural irregularity, assessor bias, or incorrect application of marking criteria).
3. Provide supporting evidence where applicable.

Appeals will be reviewed by an independent panel within **`PANEL_REVIEW_TIMEFRAME`** and the learner will be notified in writing of the outcome.

**Appeals Contact:** `APPEALS_CONTACT_NAME` — `APPEALS_CONTACT_EMAIL`
