## A.1 Programme Overview

**Programme title:** Vuka Rise: My Life, My Recipe  
**Sub-theme:** Rise Up — The Umhlobo Stew (youth prevention stream)  
**Audience:** Learners aged 8–16 (primary and lower secondary)  
**Delivery mode:** Classroom-based + interactive video module  
**Session length:** 45–60 minutes standard; 30 minutes condensed  
**Core message:** *My life is my recipe. I choose the ingredients.*

### 1.1 Learning Outcomes

By the end of a Vuka Rise session, every participant will be able to:

1. Explain the metaphor: **My life is my recipe** (choices = ingredients).
2. Identify **Spoiled Ingredients** (temptations/triggers) without glamorising substance use.
3. Demonstrate at least **two Power Moves** (Straight No; Change-Up; Strength in Numbers; The Reason).
4. Recite the collective accountability pledge with their class group.
5. Name at least one safe person and one safe channel for getting help.

### 1.2 Core Characters

| Character | Role in Programme |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Nkgono Mmatsela** | Elder/grandmother who hosts *Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen*; leads pledge in Scene 9B; embodies Ubuntu wisdom and recovery narrative |
| **Thabo** | Teenage protagonist; talented soccer player facing temptation |
| **Coach** | Supportive adult who models healthy intervention |
| **Nicotine Nelly / Alcoholic Al / Dagga Dan** | Spoiled Ingredient personifications (temptation voices) |

> **Naming note:** All references in scripts, pause screens, pledge scenes, and workbook content use **"Nkgono Mmatsela"** and **"Nkgono"** for the elder character. This replaces any earlier working title of "Gogo MaDlamini".

### 1.3 Terminology Alignment

| Vuka Rise Term | Letsema Hub Term | Shared Meaning |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Magical Ingredients | Recovery Assets | Positive strengths and supports |
| Spoiled Ingredients | Risk/Trigger Factors | Threats to wellbeing and progress |
| Power Moves | Recovery Coping Skills | Practical skills to resist and cope |
| Support Team | Care Network | Trusted individuals and services |
| The Pledge | Letsema Commitment | Collective accountability statement |
| Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen | Letsema Hub Space | Physical and metaphorical safe space |

## A.4 Video Module Reference — Scene 9B

**Scene title:** *Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen — Pledge*  
**Duration:** Approximately 8–10 minutes  
**Format:** Interactive classroom pledge video

**Scene description:**  
The scene opens in *Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen* — a warm, community kitchen setting. **Nkgono Mmatsela** addresses the class directly, guiding them through the collective accountability pledge. The scene includes:

- Recap of Magical vs. Spoiled Ingredients
- Demonstration of each Power Move by learner actors
- Class-led recitation of the Core Pledge
- Classroom add-on: "We rise together — Vuka. Rise up."

**Facilitator cue:** Press **Play** after completing the Power Moves teaching step (A.2 Step 4.3). The scene may be paused after the pledge for the Pause & Talk discussion.

## A.5 Language Apply Workflow

The video module supports multiple languages. Use the following workflow to switch languages:

1. Open the **Facilitator Mode** panel in the video module.
2. Select the desired **language tab** (e.g., isiZulu, Sesotho, Afrikaans, English).
3. A *pending* confirmation banner will appear showing the selected language.
4. Tap **Apply** to confirm the language switch.

> **Important:** Applying a language switch **restarts the video from the beginning**. Inform learners before tapping Apply: "The video will restart from the beginning."

> If you tap Apply by mistake, select the original language and tap Apply again to restore.

## B.1 Letsema Mandate

### 1.1 Mission

The Nkgono Mmatsela Letsema Recovery Hub exists to restore human dignity, purpose, and community health in Silverkraans by providing an integrated, community-led recovery environment grounded in Ubuntu and rooted in African ecological and cultural practice.

**Letsema** (Sesotho/Setswana): *a communal work party in which neighbours gather to help one another — no payment, only solidarity and reciprocal care.*

The hub operationalises Letsema as a recovery philosophy: participants give of themselves, receive from others, and collectively restore the community — including its soil, its youth, and its social fabric.

### 1.2 Vision

A Silverkraans where every person has a pathway from dependency to purpose, every family has an elder-led support anchor, and every young person grows up with a strong recipe for life.

### 1.3 Core Values

| Value | Expression in Practice |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Ubuntu** | "I am because we are." Care is collective, not transactional. |
| **Letsema** | Reciprocal community work; every participant contributes. |
| **Dignity-first** | No shaming; every person is addressed by name and role. |
| **Land-grounded** | Healing happens in relationship with soil, food, and place. |
| **Elder wisdom** | Nkgono Mmatsela embodies the hub's narrative — lived knowledge guides recovery. |

### 1.4 Target Population

- Adults aged 18+ in active or early recovery from substance dependency
- Youth aged 12–17 in the prevention/early intervention stream (*Vuka Rise — Rise Up*)
- Families and caregivers of persons in recovery
- Community volunteers and Letsema workers in skills-building pathway

### 1.5 Geographic Focus

**Primary:** Silverkraans and immediate surrounds  
**Secondary:** Broader Dependence Institute ZA programme network

## B.4 Programmatic Streams

The hub runs four interlocking programmatic streams. All streams are grounded in the Letsema philosophy and use consistent terminology aligned with the Vuka Rise prevention programme.

### 4.1 Stream 1 — Youth Prevention: Rise Up — The Umhlobo Stew

**Target:** Learners aged 8–16 in Silverkraans schools and community groups  
**Delivery:** Vuka Rise Classroom Episode (see Part A) + community reinforcement sessions  
**Frequency:** Minimum one classroom episode per term; monthly community drop-in  
**Facilitator:** Trained peer educator or teacher (NQF Level 4 Peer Counselling)

**Key activities:**
- Vuka Rise classroom session (Scene 9B — Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen)
- Power Moves skills practice
- Letsema pledge in community setting
- Youth garden project (linked to Letsema Field)
- Junior Letsema worker programme (ages 14–17)

**Outcome indicators:**
- % of learners who can name two Power Moves (post-session quick check)
- % of learners who identify a Support Team member
- Number of Vuka Rise sessions delivered per quarter

### 4.2 Stream 2 — Adult Recovery: Letsema Recovery Programme

**Target:** Adults aged 18+ in active or early recovery  
**Delivery:** Group-based therapeutic programme + individual counselling  
**Frequency:** Five days per week (Monday–Friday); day programme model  
**Facilitator:** Social worker or counsellor (NQF Level 5 or equivalent)

**Key activities:**
- Morning Letsema Circle (group check-in + accountability)
- Therapeutic group sessions (CBT-informed; trauma-sensitive)
- Individual counselling (scheduled; walk-in crisis slots available)
- Cooking-as-therapy in Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen
- Life skills modules (financial literacy, parenting, conflict resolution)
- Letsema Field work (horticultural therapy; twice weekly)

**Outcome indicators:**
- % of participants completing 30-day programme
- Relapse rates at 30, 60, and 90 days post-programme
- Employment or educational enrolment at 90 days post-programme

### 4.3 Stream 3 — Family & Caregiver Support

**Target:** Families and caregivers of persons in recovery  
**Delivery:** Weekly family group; monthly community dialogue  
**Facilitator:** Social worker

**Key activities:**
- Family group sessions (psychoeducation on dependency; communication skills)
- Caregiver wellbeing check-ins
- Monthly community dialogue (open to all Silverkraans residents)
- Letsema Kitchen — family cooking sessions (monthly)

### 4.4 Stream 4 — Vocational & Soil Restoration

**Target:** Recovery participants in the stabilised phase (30+ days); community Letsema workers  
**Delivery:** Practical skills workshops; Letsema Field operations  
**Frequency:** Three days per week (workshop); daily garden work rotation

**Key activities:**
- Accredited skills training (aligned to HWSETA unit standards where applicable)
- Letsema Field — food production; composting; soil restoration methods
- Small enterprise development (cooking, sewing, crafts)
- Community market (monthly) — participants sell produce and products

## Annex D — Dependence Institute ZA Branding Guide

### D.1 Logo Concept

The Dependence Institute ZA logo conveys **hope, resilience, and African identity** while remaining professional and reproducible in monochrome.

**Visual elements:**
- **Central motif:** A stylised rising sun emerging from a traditional three-legged pot (potjie/kggo). The sun's rays transform into the outlines of a family/community holding hands, symbolising Ubuntu.
- **Colour palette (full-colour version):**
  - Warm orange and gold — sunrise, energy, optimism
  - Deep green — growth, health, the land
  - Earth brown — grounding, tradition, the cooking pot
- **Grayscale/monochrome variant:** Solid black icon + text; no gradients; hatching or solid fills only. All printed programme materials use this variant.
- **Typography:** Clean modern sans-serif — Montserrat or Lato. "Dependence" slightly lighter weight; "Institute ZA" bold.
- **Tagline:** *Rise. Heal. Thrive.* — smaller text beneath the logo.
