# UTC Module 4 – Motivational Interviewing

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Levels:** 4 and 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 117009, US 259577
**Duration:** 8 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + role-play practicals (4 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module develops learners' proficiency in Motivational Interviewing (MI) — the primary evidence-based counselling approach used in outpatient SUD treatment. Learners practise OARS skills, learn to identify and respond to change talk, and adapt MI techniques to South African cultural contexts.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Describe the four processes of MI: Engaging, Focusing, Evoking, and Planning.
2. Demonstrate the MI spirit through partnership, acceptance, compassion, and evocation.
3. Apply OARS skills: Open questions, Affirmations, Reflective listening, and Summaries.
4. Distinguish between change talk and sustain talk and respond differentially.
5. Roll with resistance using reflection and reframing rather than confrontation.
6. Adapt MI principles to South African cultural communication norms and group treatment settings.

## Key Concepts

- **MI spirit** — a collaborative, evocative stance that honours client autonomy.
- **OARS** — the four fundamental MI micro-skills; simple vs complex reflections.
- **Change talk** — DARN-CAT (Desire, Ability, Reasons, Need; Commitment, Activation, Taking steps).
- **Sustain talk and discord** — not resistance to the counsellor, but ambivalence to change; roll rather than push.
- **Eliciting change talk** — importance and confidence rulers; looking forward/looking back; exploring values.
- **Cultural adaptation in SA** — directness, family authority, ubuntu communalism, and spirituality as MI-relevant factors; adapting OARS for Zulu, Xhosa, and Afrikaans-speaking clients.
