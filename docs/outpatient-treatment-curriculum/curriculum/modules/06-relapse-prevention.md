# UTC Module 6 – Relapse Prevention

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Levels:** 4 and 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 117151, US 117009, US 259577
**Duration:** 6 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + practical exercises (3 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module equips learners to facilitate relapse prevention planning with clients in the outpatient setting. Learners apply Marlatt's relapse prevention model, help clients identify personal high-risk situations and warning signs, and collaboratively develop written relapse prevention plans incorporating harm reduction principles.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Define lapse and relapse within a harm-reduction framework.
2. Explain Marlatt's cognitive-behavioural model of relapse.
3. Identify high-risk situations, warning signs, and personal triggers for a specific client.
4. Apply the HALT model and coping skills to manage triggers in the outpatient context.
5. Develop a personalised, written relapse prevention plan with the client.
6. Apply harm reduction principles when responding to a client who has relapsed.

## Key Concepts

- **Marlatt's relapse prevention model** — self-efficacy, coping skills, and the abstinence violation effect.
- **Apparently irrelevant decisions (AIDs)** — small, seemingly unrelated choices that create relapse risk; identifying AIDs in a client's week.
- **High-risk situations** — HALT (Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired); interpersonal conflict; negative emotional states; social pressure.
- **Warning signs** — behavioural, emotional, and cognitive precursors specific to each client's relapse pattern.
- **Emergency coping cards** — wallet-sized personalised coping strategies for high-risk moments.
- **Harm reduction** — non-judgemental response to relapse; reduce harms, re-engage treatment, review triggers and plan.
- **Social support mapping** — identifying recovery-supportive people and environments in the client's life.
