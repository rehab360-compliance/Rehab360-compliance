# UTC Module 2 – Assessment and Intake

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Levels:** 4 and 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 110009, US 119462
**Duration:** 8 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + supervised practical (4 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module equips learners to conduct structured, culturally sensitive biopsychosocial assessments and to manage the outpatient intake process. Learners practise the administration and interpretation of validated South African screening tools and develop competency in risk identification and documentation.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Distinguish between screening, brief assessment, and comprehensive biopsychosocial assessment.
2. Administer and score the DAST-10, AUDIT, and ASSIST screening tools.
3. Conduct a structured biopsychosocial intake interview.
4. Identify immediate risk factors including suicidality, withdrawal severity, and domestic violence, and initiate appropriate responses.
5. Complete an intake record that meets HWSETA documentation standards.
6. Apply POPIA-compliant consent and confidentiality procedures throughout the intake process.
7. Identify and activate referral pathways within the South African health system.

## Key Concepts

- **Screening tools** — DAST-10 (drug use), AUDIT (alcohol), ASSIST (all substances); cut-off scores; brief intervention triggers.
- **Biopsychosocial assessment domains** — substance use history; medical history; mental health; trauma; family and social history; legal; employment; spiritual/cultural.
- **Risk assessment** — Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS); CIWA-Ar for alcohol withdrawal; domestic violence safety planning.
- **POPIA consent** — purpose limitation, data minimisation, and client rights; completing consent forms.
- **SA referral pathways** — Community Health Centres (CHCs), District Mental Health teams, NGO SUD services, SANCA, DSD social workers.
