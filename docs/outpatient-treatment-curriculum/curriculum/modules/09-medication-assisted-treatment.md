# UTC Module 9 – Medication-Assisted Treatment

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Level:** 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 259577
**Duration:** 4 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + case study practical (2 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module introduces learners to the role of Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) in outpatient SUD care. Learners understand the pharmacology of MAT medications available in South Africa, communicate effectively with clients and families about MAT, and coordinate with medical and nursing staff within the outpatient MAT protocol.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Describe the role of medication in managing withdrawal, craving reduction, and co-occurring disorders in outpatient settings.
2. Explain the pharmacology and clinical use of MAT medications available in South Africa.
3. Communicate the purpose, benefits, and expectations of MAT to clients and families in accessible language.
4. Identify the boundaries of the counsellor's role in MAT and coordinate effectively with medical and nursing staff.
5. Apply monitoring protocols and document client adherence and response to MAT.
6. Describe the relevant regulatory requirements under South African law for MAT medications.

## Key Concepts

- **MAT rationale** — addiction as a chronic brain disorder; medication as a component of integrated treatment; addressing stigma around MAT.
- **SA-available MAT medications** — naltrexone (oral and injectable), disulfiram for alcohol use disorder; buprenorphine/naloxone (Suboxone) where available; methadone (limited SA access); acamprosate.
- **Alcohol withdrawal management** — CIWA-Ar monitoring; benzodiazepine protocols; referral criteria for inpatient detox.
- **Counsellor role boundaries** — counsellors support MAT adherence and psychosocial integration; medical prescribing remains with the treating doctor/nurse practitioner.
- **Client and family education** — explaining how medication works, managing expectations, addressing concerns about "trading one addiction for another".
- **NDPB and DHA regulations** — National Drug Policy; Schedule 5/6 medications; documentation and pharmacy coordination requirements.
