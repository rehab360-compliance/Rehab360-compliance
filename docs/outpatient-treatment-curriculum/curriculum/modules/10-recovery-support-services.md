# UTC Module 10 – Recovery Support Services

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Levels:** 4 and 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 117009, US 259577
**Duration:** 4 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + community mapping practical (2 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module equips learners to support long-term recovery by connecting clients with recovery capital resources and developing post-discharge recovery maintenance plans. Learners map locally available South African recovery support services and apply strengths-based, recovery-oriented language throughout their practice.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Define recovery capital and identify its four domains: personal, social, community, and cultural.
2. Map locally available recovery support resources in South Africa for a specific client catchment area.
3. Apply strengths-based and recovery-oriented language in all clinical and community interactions.
4. Explain the structure and purpose of NA, AA, and other South African peer support programmes.
5. Develop a post-discharge recovery maintenance plan in collaboration with the client and their support network.
6. Identify outcome measures for monitoring recovery beyond abstinence.

## Key Concepts

- **Recovery capital** — the breadth and depth of internal and external resources available to initiate and sustain recovery; four domains: personal (health, coping skills), social (family, friends), community (housing, employment, peer support), cultural (identity, belonging, spirituality).
- **South African peer support landscape** — Narcotics Anonymous (NA) and Alcoholics Anonymous (AA) nationwide; SANCA; ARCA; faith-based recovery communities; government DSD aftercare services.
- **Recovery-oriented language** — "person in recovery", "sustained remission"; avoiding "clean" vs "dirty" dichotomy; strengths-based framing.
- **Post-discharge plan** — step-down schedule; ongoing NA/AA commitment; emergency contacts; trigger management review; follow-up appointment schedule.
- **Family involvement** — Al-Anon, Nar-Anon; family psychoeducation; rebuilding trust.
- **Recovery outcome measures** — beyond abstinence: quality of life, social functioning, employment, housing, wellbeing scales (e.g., CHIME framework).
