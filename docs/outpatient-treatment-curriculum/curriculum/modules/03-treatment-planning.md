# UTC Module 3 – Treatment Planning

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Levels:** 4 and 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 117009, US 119462
**Duration:** 6 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + supervised practical (3 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module develops learners' ability to create person-centred, recovery-oriented treatment plans in collaboration with clients. Learners integrate findings from Module 2 assessments, incorporate the client's stage of change, and produce a documented plan that meets HWSETA standards.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Explain the principles of person-centred, recovery-oriented treatment planning.
2. Set SMART goals that reflect the client's stated priorities and stage of change.
3. Identify cultural, family, and community factors that influence the treatment plan.
4. Develop a written outpatient treatment plan including problem list, goals, interventions, and review dates.
5. Engage the client, family members, and relevant team members in the planning process.
6. Conduct a structured treatment plan review and update at defined intervals.

## Key Concepts

- **Person-centred planning** — client strengths, self-determination, and stated goals are the starting point; counsellor facilitates rather than prescribes.
- **Stages of change integration** — treatment goals match the client's readiness; interventions differ for pre-contemplation, contemplation, preparation, action, and maintenance.
- **SMART goals** — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound; applied to SUD treatment.
- **Problem–goal–intervention–review format** — standard South African clinical record structure.
- **Family and community genogram** — visualising social support and systemic risk factors.
- **Discharge criteria** — defining conditions for step-down or programme completion at the outset.
