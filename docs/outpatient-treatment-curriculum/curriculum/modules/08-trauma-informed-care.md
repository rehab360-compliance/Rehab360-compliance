# UTC Module 8 – Trauma-Informed Care

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Level:** 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 259577
**Duration:** 6 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + reflective practicals (3 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module develops learners' understanding of the relationship between trauma and SUD, and equips them to apply trauma-informed principles across all aspects of outpatient treatment practice. Learners recognise trauma indicators, respond without re-traumatisation, and maintain their own wellbeing through vicarious trauma prevention strategies.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Explain the impact of trauma on SUD aetiology, clinical presentation, and recovery trajectories.
2. Apply the six principles of trauma-informed care across all UTC modules and clinical interactions.
3. Identify behavioural and clinical indicators of trauma in outpatient clients.
4. Respond to trauma disclosures without re-traumatisation.
5. Incorporate trauma-informed language and practices into group facilitation and individual sessions.
6. Identify signs of vicarious trauma and apply self-care strategies to sustain professional wellbeing.

## Key Concepts

- **ACE study** — Adverse Childhood Experiences as predictors of SUD and mental health disorders; dose-response relationship.
- **GBV–SUD intersection** — high co-occurrence of gender-based violence exposure and SUD in South Africa; trauma as gateway to substance use.
- **Six SAMHSA principles of trauma-informed care** — Safety; Trustworthiness and Transparency; Peer Support; Collaboration and Mutuality; Empowerment, Voice and Choice; Cultural, Historical, and Gender Issues.
- **Trauma-informed assessment** — adapting language, pacing, and physical environment; not requiring trauma narrative; safety first.
- **Responding to disclosure** — grounding techniques; containing responses; reporting obligations under Children's Act and DVA.
- **Vicarious trauma** — symptoms, risk factors, and prevention; supervision and self-care as professional responsibilities.
