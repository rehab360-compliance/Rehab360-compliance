# UTC Module 7 – Co-occurring Disorders

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Level:** 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 259577
**Duration:** 6 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + case study practicals (3 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module equips NQF Level 5 learners to recognise, screen for, and apply integrated treatment principles with clients presenting with co-occurring SUD and mental health disorders. Learners understand referral pathways within the South African mental health and SUD system and apply integrated approaches in the outpatient context.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Define co-occurring disorders and explain the bidirectional relationship between SUD and mental health conditions.
2. Describe the prevalence of co-occurring disorders in the South African population.
3. Administer validated co-occurring disorder screening tools: K10, PHQ-9, and the PTSD Checklist (PCL-5).
4. Apply integrated treatment principles when working with clients with dual diagnoses.
5. Explain the role of psychiatric medication in co-occurring presentations and communicate clearly with clients about medication.
6. Identify and activate referral pathways within the South African mental health and SUD system.

## Key Concepts

- **Co-occurring disorders** — SUD co-occurring with mood disorders, anxiety disorders, PTSD, psychosis, ADHD; "which came first" is less important than integrated treatment.
- **South African prevalence** — high rates of depression, anxiety, and PTSD in SUD populations; GBV as a co-occurring risk factor.
- **Screening tools** — Kessler 10 (K10) for psychological distress; PHQ-9 for depression; PCL-5 for PTSD; administration and cut-off scores.
- **Integrated treatment** — addressing SUD and mental health concurrently within the outpatient team; the harm of treating sequentially.
- **Psychiatric medication** — explaining purpose, managing expectations, and the role of medication adherence in recovery; not a substitute for psychosocial treatment.
- **SA referral network** — District Mental Health, CMHC, NGO partners, SANCA, private psychiatric facilities; writing effective referral letters.
