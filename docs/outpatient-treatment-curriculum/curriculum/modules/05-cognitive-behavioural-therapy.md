# UTC Module 5 – Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

**Programme:** Outpatient Treatment Curriculum
**NQF Levels:** 4 and 5
**HWSETA Unit Standards:** US 117009, US 259577
**Duration:** 8 contact hours
**Delivery Format:** Facilitated group session + practical exercises (4 × 2-hour sessions)

---

## Purpose

This module introduces CBT as the core structured intervention for SUD treatment in outpatient settings. Learners develop skills to deliver CBT-informed individual and group sessions, applying the cognitive model to addiction-specific presentations and adapting techniques for low-literacy and multilingual contexts.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:

1. Explain the cognitive model of addiction using the thought–feeling–behaviour–substance use cycle.
2. Identify cognitive distortions commonly associated with SUD (e.g., catastrophising, minimisation, all-or-nothing thinking).
3. Apply basic CBT techniques: thought records, behavioural activation, and problem-solving.
4. Facilitate a structured CBT-informed group session using a session plan.
5. Adapt CBT techniques for low-literacy and multilingual South African clients.
6. Integrate CBT skills with MI techniques and treatment plan goals.

## Key Concepts

- **ABC model** — Activating event → Beliefs (thoughts) → Consequences (feelings/behaviour); Ellis/Beck cognitive model.
- **Cognitive distortions in SUD** — permission-giving beliefs ("just this once"), minimisation, craving-related catastrophising, shame-based thinking.
- **Thought records** — identifying automatic thoughts; evaluating evidence; generating alternative balanced thoughts.
- **Behavioural activation** — scheduling positive activities to disrupt avoidance and improve mood; relevant to co-occurring depression.
- **Problem-solving** — structured 5-step approach applicable to trigger management and life problems.
- **Low-literacy adaptations** — visual thought records; storytelling and narrative techniques; oral group processing.
