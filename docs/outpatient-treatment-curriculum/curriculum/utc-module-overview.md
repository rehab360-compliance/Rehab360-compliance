# UTC Module Overview – Outpatient Treatment Curriculum

This document provides a summary of all ten Universal Treatment Curriculum (UTC) modules delivered in Rehab360's outpatient SUD treatment programme. Each module entry includes learning outcomes, recommended duration, delivery format, and alignment to HWSETA unit standards.

---

## Module 1 – Addiction as a Disease

**Learning Outcomes**
- Describe the neurobiological basis of addiction and its classification as a chronic brain disorder.
- Explain the biopsychosocial model of SUD development.
- Challenge stigma and apply rights-based language in clinical and community settings.
- Identify risk and protective factors for substance use disorders.

**Key Topics:** Brain reward pathways; genetic and environmental risk factors; stages of change; South African prevalence data; PAIA/POPIA confidentiality obligations.

---

## Module 2 – Assessment and Intake

**Learning Outcomes**
- Conduct a structured biopsychosocial assessment using standardised South African tools (DAST-10, AUDIT, ASSIST).
- Document findings in a clinically appropriate intake record.
- Identify immediate risk factors including suicide, withdrawal severity, and domestic violence.
- Apply POPIA-compliant consent and confidentiality procedures at intake.

**Key Topics:** Screening vs assessment; DAST-10, AUDIT, ASSIST administration; risk and safety assessment; legal and ethical obligations; referral pathways within the SA health system.

---

## Module 3 – Treatment Planning

**Learning Outcomes**
- Develop a person-centred treatment plan collaboratively with the client.
- Set measurable short- and long-term goals aligned to the client's stage of change.
- Identify and incorporate cultural, family, and community factors in the treatment plan.
- Review and update treatment plans at defined intervals.

**Key Topics:** SMART goal setting; stages of change integration; family and community genograms; discharge planning criteria; documentation standards for HWSETA compliance.

---

## Module 4 – Motivational Interviewing

**Learning Outcomes**
- Demonstrate core MI skills: OARS (Open questions, Affirmations, Reflective listening, Summaries).
- Apply MI principles to ambivalence and resistance in the outpatient setting.
- Recognise change talk and sustain talk and respond differentially.
- Adapt MI to South African cultural communication norms.

**Key Topics:** MI spirit (partnership, acceptance, compassion, evocation); OARS practice; change talk elicitation; rolling with resistance; cultural adaptation; brief MI in group settings.

---

## Module 5 – Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

**Learning Outcomes**
- Explain the cognitive model of addiction and the thought–feeling–behaviour cycle.
- Identify cognitive distortions common in SUD (e.g., catastrophising, minimisation).
- Apply basic CBT techniques: thought records, behavioural activation, problem solving.
- Deliver a structured CBT-informed group session in the outpatient context.

**Key Topics:** ABC model; automatic thoughts and core beliefs; thought records; coping skills training; behavioural experiments; adapting CBT for low-literacy contexts.

---

## Module 6 – Relapse Prevention

**Learning Outcomes**
- Define lapse and relapse within a harm-reduction framework.
- Identify high-risk situations, warning signs, and triggers for each client.
- Apply the HALT model (Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired) and coping strategies.
- Develop a personalised relapse prevention plan with the client.

**Key Topics:** Marlatt's relapse prevention model; apparently irrelevant decisions; emergency coping cards; social support mapping; harm reduction principles; culturally relevant coping strategies.

---

## Module 7 – Co-occurring Disorders

**Learning Outcomes**
- Define co-occurring SUD and mental health disorders and explain their bidirectional relationship.
- Screen for common co-occurring disorders using validated tools (K10, PHQ-9, PTSD Checklist).
- Apply integrated treatment principles for co-occurring presentations.
- Identify referral pathways within the South African mental health and SUD system.

**Key Topics:** Prevalence of co-occurring disorders in SA; screening tools; dual diagnosis treatment models; working with psychiatric medication; integrated vs sequential treatment; referral letters and handover.

---

## Module 8 – Trauma-Informed Care

**Learning Outcomes**
- Explain the impact of trauma on SUD aetiology, presentation, and recovery.
- Apply the six principles of trauma-informed care (safety, trustworthiness, peer support, collaboration, empowerment, cultural humility).
- Identify trauma indicators and respond without re-traumatisation.
- Incorporate trauma-informed language and practices across all UTC modules.

**Key Topics:** ACE (Adverse Childhood Experiences) study; GBV and SUD intersections; trauma-informed assessment adaptations; self-care and vicarious trauma for counsellors; trauma-sensitive group facilitation.

---

## Module 9 – Medication-Assisted Treatment

**Learning Outcomes**
- Describe the role of medication in managing withdrawal, craving, and co-occurring disorders in outpatient settings.
- Explain the pharmacology and clinical use of common MAT medications available in South Africa.
- Communicate the purpose and expectations of MAT to clients and families.
- Coordinate with medical and nursing staff in the outpatient MAT protocol.

**Key Topics:** Methadone, buprenorphine/naloxone (where available in SA); naltrexone; disulfiram; alcohol withdrawal management; monitoring protocols; integrating MAT with psychosocial treatment; NDPB and DHA regulations.

---

## Module 10 – Recovery Support Services

**Learning Outcomes**
- Identify recovery capital domains (personal, social, community, cultural).
- Map locally available recovery support resources in South Africa (NA/AA, SANAC, sober living, vocational).
- Apply strengths-based and recovery-oriented language in all clinical interactions.
- Develop a post-discharge recovery maintenance plan with the client.

**Key Topics:** Recovery capital framework; South African peer support landscape; NA/AA 12-step integration; community reintegration; aftercare and step-down planning; family involvement; measuring recovery outcomes.

---

## Delivery Notes

- All modules are delivered in Afrikaans, English, Zulu, or Xhosa as required by the learner group.
- Group sessions are limited to 12 participants to ensure adequate facilitator engagement.
- Each module includes at least one role-play or case vignette exercise mapped to the relevant UTC competencies.
- Formative assessment tasks are embedded within each module; refer to the [Assessment Guide Template](../../assessment/assessment-guide-template.md) for standardised task formats.
