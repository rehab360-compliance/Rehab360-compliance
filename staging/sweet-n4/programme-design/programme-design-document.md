## 1. Programme Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
The SWEET-N4 programme prepares learners to function as peer counselors in substance abuse treatment settings. Graduates will be equipped to:
- Facilitate peer-to-peer support sessions
- Apply basic counseling techniques in recovery contexts
- Demonstrate understanding of substance dependency and treatment approaches
- Maintain ethical boundaries and professional conduct

### 1.2 Target Audience
- Individuals in recovery seeking employment in substance abuse services
- Community health workers expanding their skillset
- Support staff in rehabilitation facilities
- Volunteers in peer support programmes

### 1.3 Entry Requirements
- NQF Level 3 or equivalent (Grade 11)
- Minimum 6 months documented recovery (if applicable)
- Basic literacy and numeracy competence
- Satisfactory criminal background check
- Pre-programme interview and readiness assessment

## 2. Unit Standards Alignment

### 2.1 Core Unit Standards

#### US 117151 – Demonstrate knowledge of substance abuse and dependency
**Credits:** 10  
**Level:** 4

**Specific Outcomes:**
1. Explain the nature of substance abuse and dependency
2. Describe the bio-psychosocial model of addiction
3. Identify different categories of substances and their effects
4. Explain treatment approaches and recovery principles
5. Discuss relapse prevention strategies

**Learning Activities:**
- Classroom lectures on addiction science
- Case study analysis
- Group discussions on recovery experiences
- Research assignment on treatment modalities

**Assessment:**
- Written test (50%)
- Case study report (30%)
- Oral presentation (20%)

---

#### US 110009 – Facilitate a peer counseling session in substance abuse
**Credits:** 15  
**Level:** 4

**Specific Outcomes:**
1. Prepare for a peer counseling session
2. Conduct a peer counseling session using appropriate techniques
3. Apply active listening and empathy skills
4. Maintain professional boundaries and ethics
5. Document session outcomes and follow-up actions

**Learning Activities:**
- Role-play exercises
- Supervised practicum sessions
- Observation of experienced peer counselors
- Reflective journaling

**Assessment:**
- Practical demonstration (60%)
- Portfolio of evidence (25%)
- Reflective journal (15%)

## 3. Programme Structure

### 3.1 Duration
- **Total Programme Duration:** 12 weeks (full-time) or 24 weeks (part-time)
- **Contact Hours:** 180 hours
- **Workplace Learning:** 60 hours (minimum)
- **Self-Study:** 120 hours

### 3.2 Delivery Mode
- Face-to-face classroom instruction (60%)
- Practical workplace learning (20%)
- Self-directed study and assignments (20%)

### 3.3 Module Breakdown

| Week | Module | Unit Standard | Hours |
|------|--------|---------------|-------|
| 1-2 | Foundations of Substance Abuse | US 117151 | 30 |
| 3-4 | Bio-Psychosocial Model of Addiction | US 117151 | 30 |
| 5-6 | Treatment Approaches and Recovery | US 117151 | 30 |
| 7-8 | Peer Counseling Techniques | US 110009 | 40 |
| 9-10 | Communication Skills in Helping | US 116932 | 25 |
| 11-12 | Practical Application and Assessment | US 110009 | 45 |

## 4. Assessment Strategy

### 4.1 Formative Assessment
- Weekly quizzes and reflections
- Group discussions and participation
- Practical skill demonstrations
- Peer feedback sessions

### 4.2 Summative Assessment
- Written examination (US 117151)
- Practical competency assessment (US 110009)
- Portfolio of evidence review
- Workplace supervisor evaluation

### 4.3 Assessment Criteria
All assessments align with SAQA assessment principles:
- **Valid:** Measure intended outcomes
- **Reliable:** Consistent across assessors
- **Fair:** Accessible and unbiased
- **Authentic:** Real-world application
- **Current:** Up-to-date evidence
- **Sufficient:** Adequate proof of competence

---

## 5. Human Resources

### 5.1 Facilitators
- Must hold NQF Level 5 ODETDP or equivalent
- Minimum 2 years experience in substance abuse field
- HWSETA registration as facilitator

### 5.2 Assessors
- Must hold NQF Level 5 or higher qualification
- Registered assessor with HWSETA or ETDP SETA
- Minimum 1 year assessment experience

### 5.3 Moderators
- Must be independent from assessment process
- HWSETA-registered moderator
- Minimum NQF Level 6 qualification

- Learner guides and workbooks
- Assessment guides and tools

## 7. Quality Assurance

### 7.1 Internal Moderation
- Pre-assessment moderation of all assessment instruments
- Post-assessment moderation of 30% of learner evidence
- Moderation reports submitted before results finalization

### 7.2 External Moderation
- HWSETA external moderation (as per ETQA schedule)
- Site visits and evidence audits
- Continuous improvement based on moderator feedback

### 7.3 Learner Support
- Induction programme at intake
- Ongoing academic support and mentorship
- Access to counseling services for learners in recovery
- Accommodations for learners with disabilities

---

## 8. Workplace Learning Plan

### 8.1 Placement Sites
Learners complete 60 hours at approved sites:
- Residential rehabilitation centres
- Outpatient treatment facilities
- Peer support groups (NA, AA, SMART Recovery)
- Community health clinics with SUD services

### 8.2 Supervision
- Designated workplace supervisor at each site
- Weekly supervision meetings
- Completion of workplace logbook
- Supervisor sign-off on competence

### 8.3 Activities
- Observe experienced peer counselors
- Co-facilitate peer support sessions
- Conduct intake assessments
- Participate in case conferences
- Document client interactions (maintaining confidentiality)

---

## 9. Certification and Progression

### 9.1 Certification Requirements
Learners receive a **Statement of Results** upon:
- Achieving competence in all unit standards
- Completing workplace learning requirements
- Maintaining 80% attendance
- Submitting complete portfolio of evidence

### 9.2 Progression Pathways
Graduates may progress to:
- NQF Level 5 – Facilitator qualifications
- Higher Certificates in Counseling (HWSETA)
- Employment as peer counselor in treatment settings
- Further study in social work or psychology

---

## 10. POPIA Compliance

All learner data is collected, stored, and processed in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA):
- Learners sign informed consent at registration
- Personal information used only for educational and HWSETA reporting purposes
- Data stored securely with restricted access
- Records retained for minimum 5 years, then securely destroyed
