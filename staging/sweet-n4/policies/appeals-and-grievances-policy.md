## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This policy provides learners with a fair, transparent, and accessible process to:
- Appeal assessment decisions they believe are unfair or incorrect
- Lodge grievances about programme delivery, administration, or treatment
- Seek resolution of disputes in a timely and impartial manner

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all learners enrolled in the SWEET-N4 programme and covers:
- Assessment appeals (challenging "Not Yet Competent" decisions)
- Procedural appeals (challenging assessment processes)
- Academic grievances (programme delivery concerns)
- Administrative grievances (registration, fees, services)
- Interpersonal grievances (conduct of staff or other learners)

### 1.3 Principles
All appeals and grievances will be handled in accordance with:
- **Fairness** – Impartial consideration of all parties
- **Transparency** – Clear processes and decision criteria
- **Timeliness** – Prompt resolution within specified timeframes
- **Confidentiality** – Privacy protected throughout the process
- **Natural Justice** – Right to be heard and present evidence
- **No Victimisation** – No negative consequences for lodging legitimate appeals/grievances

## 2. Assessment Appeals

### 2.1 Grounds for Assessment Appeal

A learner may appeal an assessment decision on the following grounds:

**Valid Grounds:**
- Assessment criteria were not clearly communicated
- Assessment process was not followed correctly
- Assessor was biased or demonstrated unfair treatment
- Assessment conditions were inappropriate or inadequate
- Reasonable accommodation for disability was not provided
- Evidence was misinterpreted or not properly considered
- Marking errors or calculation mistakes occurred

**Invalid Grounds:**
- Disagreement with assessment standards or curriculum content
- Dissatisfaction with level of difficulty
- Request for additional assessment opportunities beyond policy
- Claims not supported by evidence

### 2.2 Assessment Appeal Process

#### Step 1: Informal Resolution (Within 5 working days of receiving results)

**Action:** Learner discusses concerns with the assessor

**Process:**
1. Learner requests a meeting with the assessor
2. Assessor reviews the assessment and provides clarification
3. If satisfactory, matter is resolved informally
4. If not resolved, learner may proceed to Step 2

**Timeframe:** Discussion must occur within 5 working days of receiving results  
**Outcome:** Recorded in learner file

---

#### Step 2: Formal Appeal to Programme Coordinator (Within 10 working days)

**Action:** Learner submits written appeal

**Required Information:**
- Completed Assessment Appeal Form
- Specific grounds for appeal with supporting evidence
- Copies of relevant assessment materials
- Any documentation supporting the appeal

**Process:**
1. Programme Coordinator acknowledges receipt within 2 working days
2. Coordinator reviews appeal and gathers information from assessor
3. Independent review of learner evidence by second assessor (if appropriate)
4. Decision made within 10 working days of receipt

**Possible Outcomes:**
- Appeal **upheld** – Assessment decision changed to "Competent"
- Appeal **partially upheld** – Reassessment opportunity provided
- Appeal **not upheld** – Original decision stands

**Notification:** Written decision with reasons provided to learner

---

#### Step 3: Appeal to Quality Assurance Manager (Within 15 working days)

**Action:** If not satisfied with Step 2 outcome, learner may escalate

**Process:**
1. Learner submits appeal to QA Manager in writing
2. QA Manager reviews all documentation independently
3. May convene an Appeals Panel (QA Manager + 2 independent staff members)
4. Learner may present case in person or in writing
5. Assessor may be asked to present their position
6. Decision made within 15 working days

**Possible Outcomes:**
- Appeal **upheld** – Assessment decision overturned
- Appeal **partially upheld** – Specific remedial action specified
- Appeal **not upheld** – Previous decision confirmed

**Finality:** QA Manager's decision is final within the organisation

---

#### Step 4: External Review (HWSETA/ETQA)

**Action:** If internal appeal process exhausted and learner remains dissatisfied

**Process:**
1. Learner may lodge complaint with HWSETA ETQA
2. Organisation cooperates fully with ETQA investigation
3. ETQA decision is binding on the organisation

**Contact:** HWSETA ETQA Unit  
**Website:** www.hwseta.org.za  
**Email:** [HWSETA contact email]

## 3. Academic and Administrative Grievances

### 3.1 Types of Grievances

**Academic Grievances:**
- Quality of facilitation or instruction
- Inadequate learning materials
- Insufficient learner support
- Inappropriate learning environment
- Assessment procedures not followed

**Administrative Grievances:**
- Registration or enrollment issues
- Fee disputes or refund requests
- Certification delays
- Inadequate communication from provider
- Failure to provide promised services

**Interpersonal Grievances:**
- Unfair treatment by staff
- Discrimination or harassment
- Conflict with other learners
- Breach of confidentiality
- Unprofessional conduct

### 3.2 Grievance Process

#### Stage 1: Informal Resolution (Within 10 working days)

**Action:** Learner raises concern with relevant staff member

**Process:**
1. Learner discusses issue directly with person involved
2. Attempt to resolve matter informally
3. If resolved, agreement documented
4. If not resolved, proceed to Stage 2

---

#### Stage 2: Formal Grievance (Within 20 working days)

**Action:** Learner submits written grievance

**Required Information:**
- Completed Grievance Form
- Description of issue and parties involved
- Evidence supporting the grievance
- Outcome sought by learner

**Process:**
1. Programme Coordinator acknowledges within 2 working days
2. Coordinator investigates (interviews, document review)
3. Learner and involved parties given opportunity to respond
4. Decision made within 20 working days

**Possible Outcomes:**
- Grievance **upheld** – Remedial action specified
- Grievance **partially upheld** – Some concerns addressed
- Grievance **not upheld** – No action required

---

#### Stage 3: Appeal to Management (Within 30 working days)

**Action:** If not satisfied, learner may escalate to senior management

**Process:**
1. Learner submits appeal to Academic Head/General Manager
2. Independent review of grievance and investigation
3. May include mediation between parties
4. Decision made within 30 working days

**Finality:** Management decision is final within the organisation

## 4. Learner Rights and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Learner Rights

Learners have the right to:
- Be informed of this policy at programme induction
- Lodge appeals/grievances without fear of victimisation
- Have matters dealt with fairly and confidentially
- Be heard and present evidence
- Receive written decisions with reasons
- Be accompanied by a support person (not legal representative)
- Seek external review if internal process exhausted

### 4.2 Learner Responsibilities

Learners must:
- Follow the procedures outlined in this policy
- Submit appeals/grievances within specified timeframes
- Provide accurate information and evidence
- Cooperate with investigations
- Maintain respectful communication
- Accept final decisions made through proper process
- Not make false, malicious, or vexatious complaints

---

## 5. Vexatious or Frivolous Complaints

### 5.1 Definition

A complaint may be deemed vexatious or frivolous if:
- Lodged repeatedly despite being resolved
- Lacks any factual basis or evidence
- Made with malicious intent to harm reputation
- Pursued for purposes other than genuine resolution

### 5.2 Response

The organisation may:
- Decline to investigate vexatious complaints
- Require evidence of good faith before proceeding
- Impose disciplinary action for malicious complaints

---

## 6. Record-Keeping and Confidentiality

### 6.1 Documentation

All appeals and grievances will be documented including:
- Original complaint/appeal form
- Evidence submitted by learner
- Investigation notes and findings
- Decisions and reasons
- Actions taken and outcomes

### 6.2 Confidentiality

- Appeal/grievance records kept confidential
- Shared only with those directly involved in resolution
- Stored securely with restricted access
- Retained for minimum 5 years as per SAQA requirements

### 6.3 Reporting

The Quality Assurance Manager will:
- Maintain a register of all appeals/grievances
- Provide annual summary report to management
- Identify trends and recommend improvements
- Report to HWSETA as required
