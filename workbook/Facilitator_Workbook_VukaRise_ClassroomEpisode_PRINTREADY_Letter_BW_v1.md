## VUKA RISE: MY LIFE, MY RECIPE
### Facilitator Workbook — Classroom Episode + Interactive Video + Discussion

**For:** Teachers · Social workers · Peer educators · Coaches  
**Age group:** 8–16 (adapt prompts by grade)  
**Session length:** 45–60 minutes (or 30 minutes condensed)  
**Theme:** *We rise together — collective accountability and support*  
**Print format:** US Letter · Grayscale/black-and-white

## 1) Session Purpose

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

1. Explain the metaphor: **My life is my recipe** (choices = ingredients).
2. Identify **Spoiled Ingredients** (temptations/triggers) without glamorising substances.
3. Practice at least **two Power Moves**:
   - Straight No
   - Change-Up
   - Strength in Numbers
   - (Optional) The Reason
4. Recite a **collective accountability pledge** as a group.
5. Name **safe ways to get help** (trusted adult, counsellor, clinic, helpline).

---

## 2) Safeguarding & Ground Rules (Read Aloud)

**Say:**
- "No one has to share personal stories."
- "We don't name people."
- "We speak respectfully."
- "If you are worried about yourself or someone else, you can talk to me privately after."

> **Facilitator note (private):** If a learner discloses harm or immediate danger, follow your school/organisation safeguarding protocol immediately. See Annex A of the Operational Handbook.

## 4) Session Flow (Recommended 55 minutes)

### 4.1 Welcome & Warm-up (5 minutes)

**Ask (hands up or pair-share):**
- "What is one good *ingredient* for a strong future?"

Write 3–5 answers on the board (examples: dreams, skills, practice, friends, family, health).

---

### 4.2 Mini-Story Summary (5 minutes)

**Say (briefly):**
"Thabo is a talented soccer player. He gets tempted by a Spoiled Ingredient. His performance dips. A Coach checks in with respect. Thabo returns to **Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen** to learn how to protect his dreams and support team. Then he helps a teammate too."

---

### 4.3 Teach the Power Moves (10 minutes)

Introduce each Power Move with a one-line definition and one example line:

**Straight No** — A firm, confident refusal.
- Example: "No thanks. Not for me."

**Change-Up** — Redirect energy to something positive.
- Example: "Nah—come, let's go practice / go home."

**Strength in Numbers** — Don't stand alone—get support.
- Example: "Let's leave together. Coach, can we talk?"

**The Reason (optional)** — Name your goal.
- Example: "I'm training. I can't mess up my lungs/brain."

> **Tip:** Keep lines short so learners can remember and repeat them.

---

### 4.4 Play Video — Classroom Pledge Scene 9B (8–10 minutes)

**Facilitator Mode — Language Switching (Select → Apply):**
1. Select a language tab in the video module.
2. Confirm the *pending* banner shows the chosen language.
3. Tap **Apply** (note: this restarts the pledge video from the beginning).
4. The scene opens on **Nkgono Mmatsela** leading the class through the pledge.

> If you switch language mid-session, warn learners: "The video will restart from the beginning."

---

### 4.5 Pause & Talk (2 minutes set-up + 8 minutes discussion)

**Instruction to learners:**
"You don't have to share personal stories. Just talk about what support can look like."

**3 Questions (project or read aloud):**
1. "What does 'stand with a friend' look like at school?"
2. "What does it look like on the sports field or in your team?"
3. "What does it look like online or on WhatsApp?"

**3 Safe Examples (offer if the group gets stuck):**
- "Do a Change-Up together."
- "Use Strength in Numbers: ask a trusted adult for support."
- "Send a supportive message: 'I'm with you. Let's choose our dreams today.'"

**Discussion methods (choose one):**
- Pair-share → 2 volunteers share with the group
- Small groups of 4 → each group shares 1 idea
- Whole-class brainstorm (only if time allows)

---

### 4.6 Skills Practice Role-Plays (10–12 minutes)

Split into pairs.

**Role-Play 1: "Behind the field"**
- Person A: Temptation voice ("Just one… you'll look cool.")
- Person B: Uses a Power Move

Rotate and repeat with a different Power Move.

**Role-Play 2: "Help a friend"**
- Person A: Friend feels pressured
- Person B: Uses Strength in Numbers + supportive language

**Debrief questions:**
- "Which Power Move felt easiest?"
- "Which felt hardest?"
- "What can we say that's respectful but strong?"

---

### 4.7 Closing — Class Commitment (3 minutes)

Ask learners to complete the pledge page in their Participant Workbook and tick the classroom add-on lines.

Remind:
- "We don't shame. We support."
- "If you need help for yourself or a friend, speak to a trusted adult privately."

---

## 5) Troubleshooting Guide

| Situation | Suggested Response |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Learners laugh or mock | "We keep this respectful. Someone here may be struggling." |
| Learners say "Everyone does it" | "Not everyone. And even if many do, your dream still matters." |
| Learners push for personal stories | "We keep it general in class. If you want to talk privately after, I'm here." |
| A learner discloses substance use | Thank them for trusting you. Don't interrogate in front of peers. Offer private follow-up and referrals per protocol. |

---

## 6) Quick Assessment (Non-judgmental)

At the end, learners complete in their workbook:
1. Name any two Power Moves.
2. Write one supportive message they can send to a friend.

## 7) Appendix: Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Magical Ingredients** | Dreams, talents, support, healthy choices |
| **Spoiled Ingredients** | Temptations/triggers that spoil progress |
| **Power Moves** | Refusal and coping skills usable in real life |
| **Support Team** | Trusted people who help you stay strong |
| **Nkgono Mmatsela** | The elder/grandmother character in *Nkgono Mmatsela's Kitchen* who teaches life wisdom through cooking metaphors |
